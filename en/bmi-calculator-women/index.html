BMI in women: context matters

BMI uses the same formula for everyone, but women-specific interpretation is important. Hormonal changes, life stage, and body fat distribution can change risk despite similar BMI values. Use BMI as a starting point and combine it with waist measures and body-fat estimates when needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the BMI formula different for women?+
No, the formula is the same, but interpretation can differ due to physiological differences.
Is BMI reliable during pregnancy?+
Only partially. Pregnancy-specific weight-gain guidance is more useful during that period.
What should I use with BMI?+
Waist-to-hip ratio and body-fat estimates are useful complementary metrics.

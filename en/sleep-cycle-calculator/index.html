Sleep Cycle Calculator

Find your optimal bedtime or wake time based on sleep cycles.

How it works

A full sleep cycle lasts ~90 minutes. Waking between cycles helps you feel refreshed. This calculator factors in ~15 minutes to fall asleep and recommends times aligned with complete cycles.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a sleep cycle?+
A full sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes on average and moves through light, deep and REM sleep. Four to six such cycles repeat over the night. This calculator assumes 90-minute cycles.
How much sleep do I need?+
The National Sleep Foundation (NSF) recommends roughly 7 to 9 hours of sleep per night for healthy adults. Individual needs can vary somewhat.
Why do I sometimes wake up groggy?+
Being woken in the middle of deep sleep often causes grogginess (sleep inertia). Waking at the end of a cycle, during a lighter sleep stage, is usually felt as more refreshing.
Why are 15 minutes of falling-asleep time included?+
Most people take about 10 to 20 minutes to fall asleep. The calculator adds roughly 15 minutes so the calculated times are more realistic.
What helps for better sleep?+
Professional bodies such as the American Academy of Sleep Medicine (AASM) recommend consistent sleep times, a dark and cool bedroom, and avoiding caffeine and screens before bed. Seek medical advice for persistent sleep problems.
Are the calculated times medically binding?+
No. The times are guidance based on average cycles. For persistent sleep disturbances, consult a physician or a sleep specialist.

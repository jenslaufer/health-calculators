Calculate Ovulation: How to Determine Your Fertile Days

March 29, 2026·7 min read

Whether you're trying to conceive or simply want to understand your cycle better, ovulation is the most important event in your monthly cycle.

In this article, you'll learn how to calculate your ovulation date, what the fertile window is, and which factors influence your cycle.

What Is Ovulation?

Ovulation is the moment when a mature egg is released from the ovary. It travels into the fallopian tube, where it remains fertile for about 12 to 24 hours.

Ovulation divides the cycle into two phases: the follicular phase (before ovulation) and the luteal phase (after ovulation). While the follicular phase varies, the luteal phase remains relatively constant at about 14 days.

The Luteal Phase

The time from ovulation to your next period is almost always 14 days — regardless of cycle length. That's why you can calculate ovulation by counting backwards.

How to Calculate Your Ovulation

The simplest method is the calendar method. You only need two pieces of information: the first day of your last period and your average cycle length.

Formula

Ovulation = First day of last period + Cycle length − 14 days

Example: 28-Day Cycle

Last period on March 1 → Ovulation on March 15 (day 14). The fertile window starts on March 10.

Example: 32-Day Cycle

Last period on March 1 → Ovulation on March 19 (day 18). The luteal phase stays at 14 days; only the follicular phase is longer.

The Fertile Window

Sperm can survive up to 5 days in the female body. The egg itself is only fertile for 12 to 24 hours. This creates a fertile window of about 6 days: 5 days before ovulation plus ovulation day itself.

The highest chance of conception is in the two days before ovulation and on ovulation day itself. One day after ovulation, the probability drops significantly.

Cycle Length and Variations

A "normal" cycle lasts between 21 and 35 days. The average is 28 days, but only about 13% of women actually have a 28-day cycle.

Variations of 1 to 2 days are completely normal. Larger deviations can be caused by stress, travel, illness, weight changes, or hormonal shifts.

To determine your average cycle length, track your cycle for at least 3 to 6 months and calculate the average.

Other Methods to Determine Ovulation

Basal Body Temperature

After ovulation, basal body temperature rises by 0.2 to 0.5 °C. Daily tracking reveals a pattern over several cycles.

Cervical Mucus

Just before ovulation, cervical mucus becomes clear, stretchy, and resembles raw egg white. After ovulation, it becomes thicker and cloudier.

Ovulation Tests (LH Tests)

These urine tests measure the surge of luteinizing hormone (LH) that occurs 24 to 36 hours before ovulation. They are the most accurate prediction method.

Tips for Trying to Conceive

Regular Intercourse

Every 2 to 3 days during the fertile window — especially in the 2 days before ovulation.

Take Folic Acid

Ideally start taking 400 µg of folic acid daily 3 months before planning pregnancy.

Healthy Lifestyle

A balanced diet, sufficient sleep, stress reduction, and avoiding alcohol and nicotine support fertility.

Conclusion

Calculating your ovulation is a great first step to understanding your cycle — whether for trying to conceive or natural family planning. The calendar method gives you a solid estimate, but ideally combine it with other methods like basal temperature or LH tests for more accuracy.

Remember: no calculator can predict ovulation with 100% certainty. If you have irregular cycles or have been trying to conceive for more than 12 months, speak with your gynecologist.

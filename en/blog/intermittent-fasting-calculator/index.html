Intermittent Fasting Guide with Calculator

April 2, 2026·8 min read

Intermittent fasting is not a diet in the traditional sense — it's an eating pattern that defines when you eat, not what. You alternate between eating and fasting windows to give your body time to recover.

In this article, you'll learn about the most popular protocols, science-backed benefits, and how to start intermittent fasting as a beginner.

What Is Intermittent Fasting?

With intermittent fasting, you eat only within a defined time window and fast for the rest of the day. During the fasting phase, you drink water, unsweetened tea, or black coffee — no caloric food.

The body uses the fasting time to initiate repair processes (autophagy) and activate fat burning. Unlike crash diets, your basal metabolic rate remains largely stable.

Science-Backed Benefits

  • Fat loss — Intermittent fasting lowers insulin levels and promotes fat burning.
  • Autophagy — The body breaks down and recycles damaged cell components.
  • Insulin sensitivity — Regular fasting improves blood sugar regulation.
  • Inflammation markers — Studies show reduced inflammation levels in the blood.
  • Simplicity — No calorie counting needed, just stick to the time window.

Intermittent Fasting and Calorie Deficit

Intermittent fasting alone doesn't guarantee fat loss — a calorie deficit is still key. The time window makes it easier to eat less without counting calories.

Beginner Tips

  • Start with the 16:8 protocol — skipping breakfast is the easiest entry point.
  • Drink plenty of water, unsweetened tea, or black coffee during the fasting phase.
  • Eat nutrient-dense, filling meals in your eating window — enough protein is crucial.
  • Increase fasting duration gradually — from 14:10 to 16:8 to longer protocols.
  • Listen to your body: dizziness, weakness, or irritability are signs to fast shorter.
  • Make sure you get enough macronutrients in your meals.

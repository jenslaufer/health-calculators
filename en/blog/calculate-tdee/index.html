Calculate TDEE: How to Determine Your Daily Calorie Burn

March 25, 2026·7 min read

Your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day — including basal metabolism, digestion, and physical activity.

Knowing your TDEE is the most important step whether you want to lose weight, maintain your weight, or build muscle.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) vs. TDEE

Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the energy your body burns at rest — for breathing, heartbeat, and cell regeneration. TDEE multiplies BMR by an activity factor.

TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor

The Mifflin-St Jeor Formula

The Mifflin-St Jeor formula (1990) is considered the most accurate method for calculating basal metabolic rate:

Men: BMR = 10 × Weight(kg) + 6.25 × Height(cm) − 5 × Age + 5

Women: BMR = 10 × Weight(kg) + 6.25 × Height(cm) − 5 × Age − 161

Activity Factors

Activity LevelFactor
Sedentary (office job)1.2
Lightly active (1–3 days/week)1.375
Moderately active (3–5 days/week)1.55
Very active (6–7 days/week)1.725
Extremely active (athlete)1.9

TDEE and Nutrition

Your TDEE determines your calorie goal. For weight loss, eat 500 kcal less; for muscle building, eat 500 kcal more. How to split those calories is covered in our macronutrient guide.

Your BMI can also help you determine whether losing or gaining weight makes sense.

Conclusion

TDEE is the foundation of any nutrition plan. Calculate your daily calorie burn and use the result as a basis for your macronutrient distribution.

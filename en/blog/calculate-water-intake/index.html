Calculate Water Intake: How Much Water You Need Daily

March 25, 2026·6 min read

Drinking enough water is one of the simplest and most effective things you can do for your health. But how much water is enough? The answer depends on your weight, activity level, and climate.

In this article, we'll show you the formula, the influencing factors, and practical tips for your daily routine.

The Basic Formula

Water intake (liters) = Body weight (kg) × 0.033

Example: A person weighing 70 kg needs 70 × 0.033 = 2.3 liters per day as a baseline.

This value is then adjusted by activity and climate factors.

Influencing Factors

Physical Activity

More movement means more sweat and higher water needs. Light activity increases needs by 10%, intense activity by up to 40%.

Climate

Hot, humid environments increase water needs by about 20%. In cold climates, needs decrease slightly as less sweat evaporates.

Diet

A high-protein diet increases water needs because the kidneys require more water for processing. Fruits and vegetables provide additional water.

Water Intake and Nutrition

Your water intake is closely linked to your diet. If you know your TDEE and plan your macronutrients, don't forget about fluid intake.

Conclusion

Drinking enough water is the simplest health measure. Calculate your daily water intake and spread it evenly throughout the day.

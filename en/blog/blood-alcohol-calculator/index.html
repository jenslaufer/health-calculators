Blood Alcohol Calculator: How Long Until You're Sober?

April 3, 2026·8 min read

How many drinks does it take to reach the legal limit? When are you sober enough to drive? The Widmark formula estimates your blood alcohol concentration (BAC) based on drink volume, alcohol content, body weight and gender.

This guide explains the calculation, how your body eliminates alcohol, and the key legal limits.

The Widmark Formula

Developed by Erik Widmark in 1932, this formula calculates blood alcohol concentration (BAC) in per mille (‰):

Alcohol (g) = Volume (ml) × (ABV / 100) × 0.8

BAC (‰) = Alcohol (g) / (Body weight (kg) × r)

r = 0.68 (men) | r = 0.55 (women)

The reduction factor r accounts for different body water ratios. Women have a lower average water content, so alcohol concentrates more.

Example Calculation

StepCalculation
Drink500 ml beer, 5% ABV
Alcohol (g)500 × 0.05 × 0.8 = 20 g
BAC (male, 80 kg)20 / (80 × 0.68) = 0.37‰
BAC (female, 60 kg)20 / (60 × 0.55) = 0.61‰

Alcohol Elimination

Your body eliminates alcohol at a nearly constant rate: approximately 0.15 per mille per hour. This value varies slightly between individuals but is largely independent of body weight or gender.

Current BAC = Initial BAC − (0.15 × hours)

Time until sober = Initial BAC / 0.15

Coffee, cold showers, or exercise do not speed up elimination. Only time helps.

Factors That Affect BAC

Body weight

More body mass distributes alcohol across a larger volume — resulting in a lower BAC.

Gender

Women have a lower body water ratio (55% vs. 68%), leading to a higher BAC from the same amount of alcohol.

Stomach contents

Food slows alcohol absorption but doesn't reduce the total BAC. The peak is simply delayed.

Drinking speed

Faster drinking raises BAC more quickly since elimination can't keep up with absorption.

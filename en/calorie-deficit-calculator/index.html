Frequently Asked Questions

What is a healthy calorie deficit?+
300–500 kcal/day below TDEE produces roughly 0.3–0.5 kg of fat loss per week. This pace is considered sustainable by major nutrition bodies.
What is the minimum I should eat?+
Women should not chronically eat below 1,200 kcal/day, men below 1,500 kcal/day. Lower intakes risk nutrient deficiencies and metabolic adaptation.
Why is 7,000 kcal deficit ≠ 1 kg of fat?+
The '3,500 kcal = 1 lb fat' rule is a simplification. Water shifts, glycogen depletion, and muscle changes affect the scale. Actual fat loss is usually 0.3–0.5 kg per 3,500 kcal deficit.
How do I avoid muscle loss while dieting?+
Eating 1.6–2.2 g of protein per kg of body weight and resistance training 2–4×/week sharply reduces muscle loss (ISSN guidance).
When should I take a diet break?+
After 8–12 weeks in a deficit, a 7–14-day diet break at maintenance calories can help restore hormones (leptin) and metabolic rate.
What do I do if I plateau?+
Plateaus are normal. Re-check tracking accuracy, raise activity, lower calories by ~100 kcal, or take a brief diet break.
